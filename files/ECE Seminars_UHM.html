Intelligent Open RAN: AI-Driven Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC) for Trusted Next Generation Wireless Networks


  Add to Google Calendar
Date:  Thu, April 17, 2025
Time:  10:30am - 11:30am
Location:  Holmes Hall 389; online available, check your email or contact us
Speaker:  Xiaochan Xue, Stevens Institute of Technology

Abstract

The wireless landscape is experiencing unprecedented change as networks transition to more open and intelligent frameworks. At the forefront of this evolution, Open Radio Access Networks (O-RAN) are breaking down traditional barriers, creating next generation networks where flexibility thrives and AI-driven innovation accelerates. 

This talk examines the transformative convergence of AI and Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC) within the O-RAN paradigm. This powerful combination unlocks new dimensions of network efficiency and scalability while maintaining essential trust guarantees. We demonstrate a comprehensive approach that connects cutting-edge theory with real-world deployment strategies, creating the foundation for trusted intelligent wireless infrastructure.

Bio

Xiaochan Xue is a Senior Ph.D. candidate in the Analytics and Information Security for Complex Systems Lab (AISecLab) at the Stevens Institute of Technology. She holds a B.S. in Communication Engineering from Jilin University (2017) and an M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Stevens Institute of Technology (2020). Her research focuses on AI-driven wireless communication and sensing, with an emphasis on trusted 5G, Sub-6G, NextG networks, and O-RAN security. She aims to enhance the reliability and flexibility of next-generation wireless systems.
